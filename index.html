Nuclear Power Plants and Electricity Production in Europe

Has nuclear power been greenwashed?

One of the more pressing issues facing the European Union is deciding whether or not nuclear energy should be considered a truly green energy source. To do this, countries within the Union will review the latest research trends available. Several European nations have invested a great deal into this form of electricity, and France is no exception, having the largest investment in nuclear energy of all the nations. A staggering 67% of its energy is produced from nuclear power. On the other hand, Germany, one of the European innovators in nuclear power, is pushing for a reduction in nuclear energy. They are citing nuclear waste as a point of concern for their nation. All nations in the European Union can agree that more renewable energy usage is necessary to combat climate change.

CNBC recently covered this topic in their atricle entitled "Greenwashing or viable solution? Europe has a big decision to make on nuclear power. The European Union's Climate Change Conference
is set to take place from October 31 to November 12 2021. This suggests that the topic of nuclear power will be discussed in length soon.

This map's goal is to showcase fairly recent European trends in nuclear power. This is accomplished by showing the locations of nuclear power plants throughout Europe and the share of electricity produced within each given country. The Share of electricity data and the nuclear power plant data are sourced from "Our World in Data" and PRIS. All of the data featured is from 2020.
